<p><p>The intravenous (IV) iron drugs market includes several types, primarily ferric carboxymaltose, iron sucrose, and iron dextran. Ferric carboxymaltose is a newer formulation, known for its high efficacy and safety profile, allowing for larger single doses. Iron sucrose is widely used for patients with chronic kidney disease, offering quick iron replenishment without significant side effects. Iron dextran, an older variant, is effective but may cause allergic reactions, making it less favorable in some treatments. Together, they address iron deficiency effectively in various clinical settings.</p></p>
