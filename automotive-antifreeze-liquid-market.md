<p><p>The automotive antifreeze liquid market primarily comprises three types: ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is the most commonly used antifreeze due to its excellent heat transfer properties and low cost, though it is toxic. Propylene glycol serves as a safer alternative, suitable for environmentally sensitive applications, as it is non-toxic. Glycerin, derived from natural sources, offers biodegradable properties and is used in niche applications where a green solution is preferred, particularly in certain regions.</p></p>
