<p><ul><li>0.3-1.0 Dtex</li><li>above 1.0 Dtex</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Long microfibers are categorized based on their thickness, measured in denier per filament (Dtex). Fibers with a thickness between 0.3-1.0 Dtex are considered low denier microfibers and are commonly used in textiles for their softness and moisture-wicking properties. On the other hand, fibers above 1.0 Dtex are classified as high denier microfibers and are typically used in applications requiring more durability and strength, such as automotive upholstery and industrial cleaning products. Both types cater to different market needs and preferences within the microfiber industry.</p></p>
