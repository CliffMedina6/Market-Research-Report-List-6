<p><p>Air-electrode batteries encompass technologies like zinc-air and lithium-air batteries, each leveraging oxygen from the air for reaction. Zinc-air batteries utilize zinc as the anode and produce electricity through the oxidation of zinc in the presence of oxygen, offering high energy density and lightweight properties, ideal for applications like hearing aids and electric vehicles. In contrast, lithium-air batteries utilize lithium as the anode, promising even higher energy density but facing challenges in stability and efficiency, making them more suitable for future energy storage solutions.</p></p>
