<p><p>Chemistry models are essential tools for visualizing molecular structures and interactions. The Ball and Spoke model represents atoms as spheres connected by rods, emphasizing bond angles and connectivity. Space-Filling models depict molecules as overlapping spheres, illustrating relative sizes and spatial arrangement of atoms more realistically. Other models, such as skeletal or ribbon models, provide alternative representations focusing on specific features like bond types or structural frameworks. Each type serves distinct educational, analytical, and research purposes in understanding molecular behavior and relationships.</p></p>
