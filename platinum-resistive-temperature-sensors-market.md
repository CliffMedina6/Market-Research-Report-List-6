<p><p>Platinum resistive temperature sensors are commonly categorized into two main types: wire-wound and thin film. Wire-wound sensors consist of a platinum wire wound around a ceramic or glass core, offering high accuracy and stability. On the other hand, thin film sensors have a thin layer of platinum deposited onto a substrate, providing faster response times and better repeatability. Both types are widely used in various industries for temperature sensing applications, with wire-wound sensors being preferred for high-temperature and harsh environments, while thin film sensors are suitable for more precise measurements.</p></p>
