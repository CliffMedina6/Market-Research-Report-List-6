<p><p>Immuno-oncology assays are used for studying the interaction between the immune system and cancer cells. The types of assays used in this market include immunoassays, flow cytometry, polymerase chain reaction (PCR), and next-generation sequencing. Immunoassays measure the presence and concentration of antigens or antibodies in a sample. Flow cytometry is used to analyze and quantify cells based on their surface markers. PCR is a technique used to amplify and detect specific DNA sequences. Next-generation sequencing allows for the analysis of entire genomes or targeted regions.</p></p>
