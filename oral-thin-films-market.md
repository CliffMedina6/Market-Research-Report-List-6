<p><ul><li>Sublingual Film</li><li>Fast Dissolving Dental/Buccal Film</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Oral thin films are a novel dosage form that dissolve rapidly in the oral cavity, allowing for quick absorption of drugs into the systemic circulation. There are two main types of oral thin films: sublingual films, which are placed under the tongue for absorption through the sublingual mucosa, and fast-dissolving dental/buccal films, which are placed on the gums or inner cheek for absorption through the buccal mucosa. These innovative dosage forms offer advantages such as ease of administration, improved patient compliance, and rapid onset of action.</p></p>
