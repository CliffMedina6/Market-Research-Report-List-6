<p><p>The schizophrenia therapeutics market encompasses three main categories of antipsychotic drugs: First-Generation Antipsychotic Drugs (FGAs), which primarily target dopamine receptors and are effective but often associated with significant side effects; Second-Generation Antipsychotic Drugs (SGAs), which offer a broader mechanism of action with a lower risk of extrapyramidal symptoms, making them more favorable for long-term use; and Third-Generation Antipsychotic Drugs, which represent a novel approach by stabilizing dopamine activity, offering improved efficacy and reduced side effects for patients.</p></p>
