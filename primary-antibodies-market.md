<p><p>Primary antibodies are key tools in the field of life sciences for research and diagnostics. They can be classified into two main types: polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies. Polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple immune cells and are known for their high specificity. On the other hand, monoclonal antibodies are produced from a single immune cell, ensuring high purity and batch-to-batch consistency. Both types of antibodies play a crucial role in various applications such as immunohistochemistry, flow cytometry, and Western blotting.</p></p>
