<p><p>The residential buildings occupancy sensors market includes several key types. PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors detect motion by sensing changes in infrared radiation, making them suitable for detecting human presence. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to identify occupancy and can cover larger areas. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies for enhanced accuracy and reliability. Additionally, other sensor types may include thermal and microwave sensors, each offering unique advantages for optimizing energy efficiency and security in residential settings.</p></p>
