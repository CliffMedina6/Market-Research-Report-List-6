<p><p>The external neurostimulation device market encompasses various therapeutic modalities, including Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS) and Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS). TENS utilizes low-voltage electrical currents to alleviate pain by stimulating nerves through the skin, making it popular for chronic pain management. Conversely, TMS employs magnetic fields to stimulate nerve cells in the brain, primarily used to treat depression and other neurological conditions. Both modalities are non-invasive and are gaining traction for their efficacy and ease of use in clinical settings.</p></p>
