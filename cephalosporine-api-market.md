<p><p>The Cephalosporine Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) market is categorized into four generations, each representing advancements in antibacterial activity and spectrum. First-generation cephalosporins primarily fight Gram-positive bacteria. Second-generation agents target a broader range, including some Gram-negative pathogens. Third-generation cephalosporins further enhance Gram-negative efficacy, with some also effective against resistant strains. Lastly, fourth-generation cephalosporins offer a wide spectrum and improved resistance to beta-lactamases, making them suitable for serious infections. Each generation reflects evolving therapeutic needs and microbial resistance challenges.</p></p>
