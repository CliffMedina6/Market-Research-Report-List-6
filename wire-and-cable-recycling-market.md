<p><p>The wire and cable recycling market encompasses various methods for extracting valuable materials from obsolete electronics. Manual peeling involves labor-intensive removal of insulation using hand tools. Mechanical peeling employs machines to strip wires automatically, increasing efficiency. Mechanical crushing breaks down cables into smaller pieces for easier processing. The chemical method uses solvents to dissolve insulation while preserving metals. Freezing involves freezing cables to make insulation brittle, allowing for easier removal. Each method offers unique benefits depending on operational scale and material type.</p></p>
