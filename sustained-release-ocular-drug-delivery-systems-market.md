<p><p>Sustained release ocular drug delivery systems offer various types of formulations like liposomes, microcapsules/nanocapsules, microspheres/nanospheres, and implants. Liposomes are lipid-based vesicles that can encapsulate drugs for controlled release. Microcapsules/nanocapsules are tiny particles containing drug molecules. Microspheres/nanospheres are small spherical particles that release drugs slowly. Implants are solid devices that are placed in the eye to continuously release medication. These different types of sustained release systems provide options for targeted and prolonged drug delivery to treat ocular diseases.</p></p>
